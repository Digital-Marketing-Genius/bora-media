The challenge

  • No conversion tracking—ad spend wasn’t tied to bookings or leads, so ROI was invisible.
  • Poor campaign setup—inefficient structures and broad targeting wasted budget.
  • Overall underperformance—competitors outpaced the hotel across paid search.

Key insights

  • Desktop users booked the highest-value stays: ~45% of spend drove ~59.6% of revenue.
  • Mobile dominated click-volume (71.3%) but skewed discovery-stage—great for scale, weaker for closing.
  • Once revenue tracking was live, decisions could be made to value—not clicks.

Strategy

  • Rebuild measurement end-to-end (GA4 + GTM): bookings, leads, and revenue stitched to channels.
  • Shift budget to conversion-driven Performance Max + high-intent Search built around hotel bookings & catering.
  • Device-aware planning: desktop for high-value bookings; mobile to scale discovery efficiently.

Execution

  • Full account audit → remove waste, tighten match types, and align ad copy with booking intent.
  • Continuous bid & audience optimization; device modifiers prioritized desktop in revenue-dense segments.
  • Looker Studio dashboards and Hotjar insights closed the loop for weekly decisions.

Device-level insights

How the data sharpened our optimization

What we saw

  • Mobile drove 71.3% of clicks—cheap scale and strong top-of-funnel discovery.
  • Computers generated ~59.6% of revenue from ~45% of spend—higher booking values.
  • Tablets & TV screens contributed <1%—de-prioritized to reduce noise.

How we reacted

  • Applied device bid adjustments to tilt budget toward desktop during high-value windows.
  • Used mobile for efficient reach—creative and landing flows optimized for pre-booking interest.
  • Performance Max asset groups split by device signals; Search campaigns layered with device & audience combos.
