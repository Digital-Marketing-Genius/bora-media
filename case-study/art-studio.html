The challenge

  • Overall business underperforming, with inconsistent bookings and limited cashflow predictability.
  • Studio didn’t believe digital could drive real revenue.
  • No conversion tracking—ad spend wasn’t tied to bookings or class registrations.
  • Poor targeting and campaign structure led to wasted spend and low intent traffic.

Key insights

  • Demand existed—search volume and CTR growth signaled strong local intent (1.4M impressions; 28.1K clicks).
  • When tracked properly, conversion economics worked: $20.54 CPA with $240K value from $25.7K spend.
  • On-site clarity mattered: pages answering “class fit, schedule, and outcomes” converted far higher than generic pages.

Strategy

  • Measurement-first: GA4 + GTM + Microsoft Clarity to capture conversions and qualitative UX signals.
  • Conversion-first Google Ads plan focused on high-intent searches (classes, workshops, private sessions) and location modifiers.
  • Offer-matched landing pages with schedules, proof, and friction-reduction (clear pricing, limited seats, FAQs).

Execution

  • Campaign audit & rebuild: tightened keyword themes, negatives, geo-radius, and ad extensions; rebuilt conversion actions.
  • Weekly optimization loops on bids, query sculpting, and creative; A/B tests on headlines emphasizing outcomes (skill growth, portfolio pieces, community).
  • Clarity heatmaps & session replays guided UX tweaks—simpler forms, clearer class levels, and mobile schedule buttons.

Evidence
